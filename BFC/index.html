About Us

Dr. Stephanie Perry

Dr. Stephanie Perry’s mission is to provide personalized, quality chiropractic care with special attention to pregnant mothers and children. She strives to give her patients more fulfilled and enjoyable lives and to educate them about chiropractic care so they, in turn, can educate others. Dr. Stephanie is committed to maximizing the quality of life within her community and genuinely likes to help people! She is determined to increase the awareness of the benefits of Chiropractic care in the community and at large.

Dr. Stephanie is a graduate of the first chiropractic college, Palmer College of Chiropractic in Davenport, IA. She has her Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from Cal State University of Chico where she was also a member of the collegiate softball team. She is currently working on a diplomate in Pediatrics. Dr. Perry is a member of the International Chiropractic Pediatric Association (ICPA) and is Webster Certified.

Dr. Stephanie is available to speak to your company, school, or club on health related topics such as, family wellness topics, whole foods supplementation and purification, as well as, various other musculoskeletal conditions.

Dr. Stephanie lives in Bakersfield, Ca with her husband Dr. William E. Perry and their children Memphis, River, and Savannah. She spends her free time with her family and Labrador retriever Montana, camping, and fishing. She also enjoys fitness classes, knitting, sewing, and baking.

Dr. William Perry

Dr. William Perry graduated from Palmer College of Chiropractic in June 2008. Soon after, he opened Keystone of Health Chiropractic in Eagle, Idaho, along with his wife Dr. Stephanie Perry. They moved to Redding in March 2012, in order to join Dr. Todd Royse at Alive Spine & Wellness.

After a standout high school football career, Perry sustained a serious injury during his senior season. He lost all function in his left leg and believed he would never get the chance to play football at the collegiate level. Physician prescriptions included drug regimens that saw little progress, and after two weeks of particularly excruciating pain, Perry’s mother took him to see Dr. Steve Allen, a local chiropractor.

Dr. Allen took a new approach, focusing on structural alignment. 3 weeks and countless adjustments later, Perry began to see progress. By the end of treatment, he had fully recovered and went on to receive scholarships for both football and track at Shippensburg University. He would later successfully walk-on to Division I powerhouse Penn State University.

Dr. Perry attributes chiropractic care with giving him his life back, and accordingly, he decided to enter the field himself in 2004. After learning about the body’s profound ability to self-regulate, he decided to eliminate the numerous drugs he had used continuously since the injury. Within a year, Perry was completely drug free, needing none of the artificial supplementation on which he had so heavily depended.

Dr. Perry believes in research-focused chiropractic care. He is passionate about seeing individuals restored to health and giving Alive Spine & Wellness patients an experience they will never forget. Dr. Perry also believes strongly in giving back to the community. He volunteers regularly with the Good News Rescue Mission, as well as the San Quentin federal prison.

Dr. Perry’s children, Memphis and River, are the pride and joy of his life. On a typical weekend, you’ll probably find him hiking, fishing, camping with the family, coaching football, raising chickens, or studying up on the latest in chiropractic techniques.

Our Philosophy

"Health is all about how your body is functioning. Chiropractic allows you to achieve and maintain health by aligning the spine and removing interference, ultimately improving function. When your body is functioning at 100%, you have the innate ability to adapt to anything that occurs in your environment and you can maintain optimal health."
